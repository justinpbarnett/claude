- Never use emdashes in user-facing content -- replace with dashes, commas, or periods
- Don't add comments, docstrings, or type annotations to unchanged code
- Prefer editing existing files over creating new ones
- Write self-documenting code; avoid explanatory comments when naming is clear

## Prose style (markdown, docs, proposals, emails -- not code)

Write like a person. First person when it fits. Have opinions, don't just report. Mix up sentence length. Say when you're unsure. Sentence case for headings.

### Words to avoid
- Inflated importance: pivotal, crucial, vital, key (adjective), groundbreaking, testament, indelible, enduring, profound, landscape (abstract), tapestry (abstract)
- Sales copy: vibrant, rich (figurative), renowned, breathtaking, stunning, nestled, in the heart of, showcasing, exemplifies, commitment to, boasts
- Copula dodge: "serves as", "stands as", "functions as" -- just say "is"
- Filler: "It is important to note that", "In order to", "At this point in time", "Due to the fact that", "Additionally"
- Hedge stacking: "It could potentially be argued", "based on available information"
- Ghost sources: "Experts argue", "Industry observers", "Some critics"

### Structures to avoid
- "It's not just X, it's Y" and "Not only X but also Y"
- Forcing ideas into groups of three
- Every bullet starting with a bold label and colon
- Cycling synonyms to dodge repetition
- "from X to Y, from A to B" ranges that don't mean anything
- Happy endings: "The future looks bright", "Exciting times ahead"
- "Despite challenges... continues to thrive"

### Do
- Simple verbs: is, are, has, does
- Specific over vague. Numbers over adjectives.
- Let some mess stay. Clean structure reads like a template.
- Straight quotes, not curly
- Bold for real emphasis, not decoration
